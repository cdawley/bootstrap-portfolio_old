About Me

My name is Christian Dawley. I'm not sure what your name is, but if you're reading this you've reached my page. I've been interested in writing code since before I was a teenager (perhaps its worth mentioning that I'm not longer in my twenties, let alone a teenager, so it's been a little while now.) Some guy at the local library in the town where I was raised offered to teach me a little HTML, and for whatever reason it stuck. Until recently, writing code hasn't been much more than a hobby. I've worked a number of dull and uninspiring jobs (restaurants, warehouses, etc.) and attended some community college courses (both in the New Hampshire seacoast and one summer in Santa Fe, New Mexico.)

A Statistics professor at a local community college told me a about a "Coding Bootcamp" offered through the University of New Hampshire. I decided to check it out. I'm currently enrolled (it's my reason for making this page, actually) and as if the idea of writing code professionally wasn't enticing to begin with, I grow more and more fond of the idea with every new piece of knowledge I acquire. Since first being introduced to the internet, the amount of attention I've given it, and more specifically coding, has ebbed & flowed: anywhere between a dull trickle and a roaring deluge. I'm proud to say being a developer something I'm happily working to be
